Given Tree:

        8
       / \
      3   10
     / \
    1   6
Inorder Traversal (Left → Root → Right)

1, 3, 6, 8, 10

Preorder Traversal (Root → Left → Right)

8, 3, 1, 6, 10

Postorder Traversal (Left → Right → Root)

1, 6, 3, 10, 8

Why does Inorder Traversal of a BST produce sorted output?

In a BST, all values in the left subtree are smaller than the root and all values in the right subtree are larger. Since inorder traversal visits Left → Root → Right, the values are visited in ascending order.
